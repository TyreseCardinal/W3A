Felis Catus

Felis catus, commonly known as the domestic cat, is a small carnivorous mammal that has been a companion to humans for thousands of years. Belonging to the family Felidae, domestic cats are known for their agility, sharp retractable claws, and keen senses. They have a diverse range of coat colors and patterns, reflecting centuries of selective breeding.

Domestic cats are valued for their ability to control pests and for their affectionate nature, making them popular pets. Scientifically named Felis catus, these feline companions exhibit a wide range of behaviors, from playful antics to independent exploration. They have adapted well to various environments, living in homes, farms, and urban landscapes.
